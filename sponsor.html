The Event

Open Data Day DC is a yearly, two-day event in Washington, DC.

It is coordinated to occur on the same weekend as more than 100 other Open Data Day events throughout the world.

Goals:

  • Grow DC’s open data community
  • Teach open data, data science, civic hacking, and related skills
  • Do so in a welcoming and professional manner
When
March 4–5, 2016
Where
Washington, DC
(venue TBD)
Who
350 professionals and hobbyists who use open data to make the world better.

For more see http://dc.opendataday.org.

The Program

Friday

Interactive workshops geared toward data and policy professionals.

Last year’s workshops included introductions to open data, data science, hacking, mapping, APIs, and more. Participants with all levels of experience (including none) are welcome.

Saturday

Full-day hackathon session to build open data prototypes, plus more workshops.

Hackathon projects mix open data with real world issues, from public education here in the District to road safety in the Philippines. Code for Nepal launched at our event in 2014!
