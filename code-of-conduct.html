#OpenDataDay #DC

Code of Conduct

Along with many other technology-related professional events, we are adopting a code of conduct for Open Data Day in DC.

We want everyone to have a good time at Open Data Day. To that end, we are setting a few basic guidelines, and if you have any questions, comments, or concerns, please do not hesitate to reach out to any of the organizers.

Guiding Principles for Awesomeness

Because there are already excellent codes of conduct for existing events that bring people together to learn and foster community, we will just refer to those.

The Hackbright Academy's code of conduct expands on four simple main guidelines:

Be respectful. Be thoughtful. Be open. Be awesome.

PyCon 2013’s code of conduct echoes some of the same feelings:

Be kind to others. Do not insult or put down other attendees. Behave professionally.

Harassment

Because technology conferences have had a history of harassment problems, we want to address this specifically. The 2014 Lean Startup Conference defined harassment this way:

When we say 'harassment,' we’re talking about unwelcome or hostile behavior, including speech that intimidates, creates discomfort, or interferes with a person’s participation in the conference (speaker presentations fall under this category and should not use images or examples that would violate the code of conduct); unwelcome physical contact; unwelcome sexual attention; deliberate intimidation; and stalking. Sponsors should not use sexualized images or activities, and sponsor representatives (including volunteers) should not use sexualized clothing/uniforms/costumes.

You can expect a swift response to any harassment at our event.

Thanks to Sarah Rigdon and the TechLady Hackathon and Training Day for allowing us to adapt their compilation of codes of conduct.
